# 7thsense Undeniable Offer Architecture

## The Josh Principle

The offer must be structured so the buyer cannot logically say no.

**Josh's structure:**
- "You spend $50K/month on ads."
- "I reduce it to $40K with better performance."
- "I take half the $10K saving for 12 months."
- "You keep everything after that."

**Buyer's risk:** ZERO. Worst case: they keep spending $50K (status quo). Best case: they save $5K/month net AND get better results.

**Why it works:** The buyer is not being asked to spend money. They're being asked to stop losing money. Josh gets paid from the delta — money the client never had before.

## The 7thsense Equivalent Structures

### Structure A — Risk Reversal (Cold Outreach)

**Script:**
"I build the site first. You look at it. If you love it, you pay. If you don't — no cost, no obligation."

**Why it works:** Removes all financial risk. The prospect risks 30 minutes of their time. You risk 3-4 hours of build time. But if the demo is good, conversion rate is high because they've already seen the product.

**When to use:** First contact with a cold prospect. Always the default for 7thsense outreach.

**Economics:** If 1 in 5 demos converts at R7,500, your effective rate is R1,500/hour of demo building. If 1 in 3 converts, it's R2,500/hour. The math works even at low conversion rates.

### Structure B — Performance Guarantee (Warm Prospects)

**Script:**
"If you don't get at least [X] new [patients/clients/enquiries] through the site in the first 90 days, I work for free until you do."

**Why it works:** Transfers all performance risk to you. The prospect can only win — either they get results, or they get free work.

**When to use:** Prospect likes the demo but is hesitant. They say "looks great but will it actually work?"

**[X] by niche:**
- Dental: 5 new patient enquiries in 90 days
- Law: 3 new consultation requests in 90 days
- Aesthetics: 8 new booking enquiries in 90 days

**Your actual risk:** Low. A properly built conversion-focused site with real reviews and CTAs will generate enquiries. The guarantee costs you nothing if the build quality is high.

### Structure C — Revenue Share (High-Value Niches)

**Script:**
"I build and run your entire [patient/client] acquisition system. I get paid R[X] per new [patient/client] I generate above your current baseline."

**Why it works:** Perfectly aligned incentives. You only make money when they make money. No upfront cost to the client. Unlimited upside for both parties.

**When to use:** Law firms (one case = R50,000+), high-end aesthetics, any niche where one client LTV exceeds R10,000.

**Example calculation (dental):**
- Current: 20 new patients/month
- After site launch: 30 new patients/month
- Delta: 10 new patients/month
- Your fee: R500 per new patient above baseline
- Monthly revenue: R5,000
- Annual revenue: R60,000
- Client's additional revenue: 10 patients × R6,700 LTV = R67,000 in LTV per month
- Client ROI: 1,340% (they pay R5K to gain R67K in patient value)

### Structure D — Savings Model (Josh's Exact Adaptation)

**Script:**
"Based on your Google Maps listing and the searches happening in your area, you're losing approximately R[X] per month in missed leads. I fix the conversion layer — your site, your booking flow, your follow-up. I get paid a portion of the revenue increase over 12 months. If there's no increase, you owe nothing."

**Why it works:** You're not asking them to spend. You're asking them to stop losing. The money already exists — it's just leaking out through a bad website.

**When to use:** Businesses running Google Ads or Local Services Ads with weak landing pages. The ad spend is proof of the problem AND proof of the budget.

**Example (law firm running Google Ads):**
- Current monthly ad spend: R15,000
- Estimated current conversion rate: 2%
- After site fix estimated conversion rate: 5%
- Monthly leads gained: 15 additional consultations
- At R20,000 per case value: R300,000 in potential case value
- Your fee: R10,000/month (3.3% of value created)
- Client net gain: R290,000/month in potential case value minus R10,000 fee

## Offer Scorecard

Before presenting ANY offer, score it against these 5 questions:

| Question | If the answer is wrong | Action |
|----------|----------------------|--------|
| Does the buyer risk anything financially? | Yes = bad | Add guarantee or risk reversal |
| Is the outcome specific and measurable? | No = bad | Quantify: "5 new patients" not "more visibility" |
| Is the ROI obvious without explanation? | No = bad | Add the math: "Two patients = paid for itself" |
| Could someone say no and feel smart? | Yes = bad | The offer isn't good enough — restructure |
| Does it have a name that communicates the outcome? | No = bad | Rename around the result, not the deliverable |

**All 5 must pass before the offer goes out.**

## Offer Progression (the natural sales sequence)

1. **Free demo** (Structure A) — gets attention, proves capability
2. **Starter sale** (R7,500 with satisfaction guarantee) — first revenue
3. **Performance guarantee upsell** (Structure B) — converts hesitant prospects
4. **Revenue share proposal** (Structure C) — for high-value clients after trust is established
5. **Retainer** (R2,500/month) — ongoing revenue after proof of results

Each stage builds on the trust from the previous one. Never jump to Structure C or D on first contact — earn the right to propose aligned incentives.

## Anti-Patterns (offers that fail)

- "Here's our pricing page" — makes it about cost, not value
- "We charge R7,500 for a website" — commodity framing invites comparison
- "Let me know if you're interested" — no urgency, no specificity
- "We offer three packages" — overwhelming choice, decision paralysis
- "Check out our portfolio" — you're selling outcomes, not showing off
- Leading with features instead of the problem they're experiencing
