# 7thsense Hormozi Offer Design

## Core Principle: The Grand Slam Offer

An offer so good people feel stupid saying no.

**The Value Equation:**
```
Value = (Dream Outcome × Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay × Effort & Sacrifice)
```

To maximise value:
- **Increase** Dream Outcome — make the result bigger
- **Increase** Perceived Likelihood — make them believe it will work
- **Decrease** Time Delay — deliver results faster
- **Decrease** Effort & Sacrifice — make it require nothing from them

## Offer Design Process

### Step 1 — Identify the Dream Outcome
Not what we deliver. What they actually want.

| Niche | What we deliver | What they actually want |
|-------|----------------|----------------------|
| Dental | Website | Full waiting room, new patients every week |
| Aesthetics | Website | Booked out practice, clients rebooking every quarter |
| Law | Website | Phone ringing with qualified cases, not tire-kickers |
| Plumber | Website | Emergency callouts coming in, not chasing work |

**Rule:** If you catch yourself describing the deliverable instead of the dream — rewrite.

### Step 2 — List Every Problem Between Them and the Dream
The prospect doesn't just need a website. They have a CHAIN of problems:

**Dental example:**
1. People search "dentist near me" but can't find them online
2. Google Maps listing exists but website link is broken or missing
3. When someone does find the site, it looks unprofessional
4. No way to book online — have to call during business hours
5. No social proof visible — reviews exist on Google but not on the site
6. Mobile experience is terrible — 80% of visitors are on phones
7. No after-hours contact option
8. No follow-up system for leads who enquire but don't book

### Step 3 — Solve Every Problem in the List
A Grand Slam Offer solves the ENTIRE chain, not just one link:

| Problem | Our Solution |
|---------|-------------|
| Can't be found | SEO-optimised site with local business schema |
| Broken website | New conversion-focused site, live in 5 days |
| Looks unprofessional | Premium design matching their brand identity |
| Can't book online | Tally.so form + click-to-call + WhatsApp |
| No social proof | Google reviews with full wordmark, prominently displayed |
| Bad mobile experience | Mobile-first build, floating CTA bar |
| No after-hours option | WhatsApp auto-reply + contact form |
| No follow-up | Make.com automation — form → email notification |

### Step 4 — Price Based on Value Not Time
**Never say:** "The website costs R7,500."
**Always say:** "Two new patients and this pays for itself. At R6,700 lifetime value per patient, the ROI is 1,800% in year one alone."

The price should feel like a rounding error compared to the value.

### Step 5 — Add Guarantees That Remove All Risk

**Performance Guarantee (strongest):**
"If you don't get at least 5 new enquiries through the site in the first 90 days, I work for free until you do."

**Satisfaction Guarantee:**
"If you're not happy with the site within 30 days of go-live, full refund."

**Risk Reversal (best for first contact):**
"I build it first. You look at it. If you love it, we talk. If not, no cost."

**Revenue Share (highest value niches):**
"I take R500 per new patient above your current baseline. You only pay when you're making more money."

### Step 6 — Name the Offer Around the Outcome

| Bad Name | Good Name | Why |
|----------|-----------|-----|
| Starter Website | The New Patient System | Describes what they get, not what we do |
| Growth Package | The Booked-Out Practice Programme | Aspirational and specific |
| Full System | The Consultation Engine | Action-oriented, implies automation |
| Monthly Retainer | The Patient Pipeline | Ongoing value, not ongoing cost |
| Website Redesign | The First Impression Upgrade | Names the actual problem being solved |

## Average Offer vs Grand Slam Offer

### Average Agency Offer (what everyone does):
- "We'll build you a 5-page website"
- Price: R7,500
- Guarantee: none
- Timeline: 2-4 weeks
- Risk: 100% on the buyer
- Buyer thinks: "That's a lot of money for something I'm not sure will work"

### Grand Slam Offer (what we should do):
- "The New Patient System — your phone ringing more within 30 days, or we work free until it does"
- Price: R7,500 (same price, 10x the perceived value)
- Guarantee: performance-based — 5 new enquiries in 90 days or free work until achieved
- Timeline: demo in 24 hours, live in 5 days
- Risk: 100% on us
- Buyer thinks: "I literally cannot lose. If it doesn't work, they keep working for free. If it does work, it pays for itself with one patient."

## Value Equation by Niche

### Dental
- **Dream Outcome:** 10+ new patients per month, full schedule, no empty chairs
- **Perceived Likelihood:** HIGH — we show them a demo with their actual reviews and data
- **Time Delay:** LOW — demo in 24 hours, live in 5 days, first patient within 30 days
- **Effort:** NEAR ZERO — we handle everything, they just approve
- **Value Score:** Very High
- **Pitch:** "Two new patients and this pays for itself forever."

### Law
- **Dream Outcome:** Phone ringing with qualified cases, not cold callers
- **Perceived Likelihood:** HIGH — demo shows professional, credible site with real credentials
- **Time Delay:** LOW — demo in 24 hours, live in 5 days
- **Effort:** NEAR ZERO
- **Value Score:** Extremely High (one case = R50,000+)
- **Pitch:** "One new case from this site and the investment is invisible."

### Aesthetics
- **Dream Outcome:** Booked out 3 weeks in advance, clients rebooking automatically
- **Perceived Likelihood:** HIGH — demo matches their premium brand feel
- **Time Delay:** LOW
- **Effort:** NEAR ZERO
- **Value Score:** Very High
- **Pitch:** "Two regular clients and this pays for itself every year, on repeat."
