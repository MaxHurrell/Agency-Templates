# 7thsense Niche Intelligence — Medical Spas & Aesthetics Clinics

## Why This Niche
High value, image-driven, and massively underserved in terms of web quality. Clients spend $500–$5,000+ per treatment plan. A website that looks as good as the results builds instant credibility. Bad websites lose clients to competitors immediately because in aesthetics, how you present yourself IS the product.

Best sub-niches: Botox and injectables, laser treatments, skin rejuvenation, body contouring, medical-grade facials.

## Visitor Mindset
- Aspirational but anxious — they want to look better but fear looking overdone or unnatural
- Trust is everything — they are putting their face in someone's hands
- Social proof driven — before/after results and reviews matter more than credentials
- Visual first — they judge the quality of the work by how the website looks
- Private — many clients do not want people to know they are getting treatments
- Repeat customer potential — aesthetics clients return every 3-6 months if they trust you

## Hero Section Rules
- Headline: Outcome-focused, aspirational — "Look Fresh, Not Frozen" style taglines work better than generic "Welcome to our clinic"
- Real photo of the practitioner or a beautiful treatment result — never stock images of syringes or generic spa photos
- Warm, luxury tone — rose gold, cream, soft neutrals or deep sophisticated tones
- One CTA: "Book a Consultation" or "Book Now" — never "Contact Us"
- Subheadline: Specific treatment or specialisation + location
- If reviews exist: Google rating badge is mandatory — social proof is critical in aesthetics

## Must-Have Sections (in order)
1. Hero — practitioner photo or aspirational imagery, outcome headline, book now CTA, Google rating
2. Trust strip — years experience, Google rating, review count, treatments/clients served
3. About the practitioner — credentials but written warmly. Philosophy of natural results. Why they do this work.
4. Services — image cards per treatment, never icon-only. Beauty imagery essential.
5. Before/after or results — if available. Most powerful trust signal in aesthetics.
6. Reviews — Google reviews with wordmark. Aesthetics clients read reviews obsessively.
7. How it works — "What to expect at your first visit" — reduces anxiety about the unknown
8. FAQ — addresses common fears: "Will I look natural?", "Does it hurt?", "How long does it last?"
9. Contact — book consultation form. Response time commitment helps.

## Trust Strip Stats (aesthetics)
- Years of experience
- Google rating
- Review count
- Number of treatments or clients served
Never use location names as stats. Never use Saturday hours as a stat.

## CTA Rules
- Primary: "Book a Consultation" or "Book Now"
- Never "Contact Us" — too passive for aesthetics
- Floating mobile bar: Call + WhatsApp both acceptable — aesthetics clients often prefer WhatsApp for discreet enquiries
- Add online booking if available

## Copy Rules
- Warm, confident, aspirational
- Lead with outcomes and how the client will feel
- Never clinical or cold — even medical treatments should feel luxurious
- Address fears directly: "natural results", "subtle enhancement", "no one will know"
- Speak to both men and women unless practice is explicitly female-focused

## Visual Rules
- Warm colour palettes: rose gold, cream, champagne, blush, deep teal, warm charcoal
- Never cold clinical white — always add warmth
- Photography: skin close-ups, serene treatment spaces, confident glowing people
- Typography: elegant but modern — never ornate serifs

## What to Avoid
- Stock images of syringes, needles, or medical equipment
- Cold clinical photography
- Generic "beauty" stock photos of models who clearly haven't had treatments
- Overly medical tone — this is luxury wellness, not a hospital
- Listing every single treatment brand name in the navigation (Juvéderm Volbella XC etc)
- Saturday hours as a trust stat if Saturday is closed or by appointment only
