# 7thsense Strategic Intelligence
## The Josh Framework — End to End

*This skill is the brain. Every other strategic skill (signal-detection, opportunity-scanner, roi-calculator, hormozi-offer, undeniable-offer, offer-architect) is a tool this skill orchestrates. When this skill is active, think like Josh — not like an agency.*

---

## SECTION 1 — THE JOSH MINDSET

These are not principles. They are lenses. Every decision, every message, every build must pass through all five simultaneously.

### Lens 1: We find bleeding, we stop it
We are not selling websites. We are not selling design. We are finding businesses that are provably losing money right now — and offering to stop the loss. The website is a mechanism, not the product. The product is recovered revenue.

If you cannot point to a specific monthly loss in rands, you do not have a prospect. You have a guess.

### Lens 2: Problem first, always
Every prospect interaction starts with their problem — never with what we do. The first sentence of every message must describe something they are losing, not something we can build.

**Wrong:** "Hi, I build websites for dental practices."
**Right:** "Your services page returns a 500 error. Every patient who clicks it leaves."

The prospect must feel understood before they feel sold to.

### Lens 3: The offer must be mathematically undeniable
Structure every offer so that saying no is irrational. This means:
- The buyer risks nothing (we build first, they pay only if they want it)
- The ROI is obvious (two new patients and it pays for itself)
- The cost of inaction is higher than the cost of action
- There is a named guarantee with specific terms

If a smart, sceptical business owner can logically justify saying no — the offer is not good enough. Redesign it.

### Lens 4: We get paid from results, not work
The value we create is measured in patients gained, enquiries received, calls answered — not in hours worked or pages built. Frame every price as a fraction of the value delivered.

R7,500 sounds expensive. "Two new patients and it's paid for itself" does not.

### Lens 5: The demo is proof we understand their business
The demo is not a sales tool. It is evidence. It says: "I researched your practice, found your real data, understood your brand, and built something that works — without you asking me to." That level of effort, delivered for free, creates an obligation that no pitch can match.

## SECTION 2 — THE FIVE QUESTIONS

Before any prospect interaction — outreach, demo build, follow-up, call prep — answer ALL FIVE. If any answer is missing or vague, stop and find the data.

### Question 1: What is the specific, quantifiable problem?
Not "their website is bad." Specific: "Their services page returns a 500 error, they have no contact form, and their phone number is invisible on mobile."

### Question 2: What signal proves they have this problem RIGHT NOW?
Not assumed — observed. "They appear in Google search results for 'dentist bellville' but their landing page scores 2/10 on our assessment: no form, no CTA, no phone on mobile, services page broken."

### Question 3: What is the monthly cost in rands?
Show the calculation. Every number must be traceable to a source.

**Template:**
```
Monthly visitors (estimated): [X]
Current conversion rate (weak site): [Y]%
Potential conversion rate (optimised): [Z]%
Conversion gap: [Z - Y]%
Lost patients/month: [X] × [gap] = [N]
Patient LTV: R[LTV]
Monthly missed lifetime value: [N] × R[LTV] = R[TOTAL]
```

If running Google Ads, add:
```
Monthly ad spend: R[spend]
Waste rate on weak landing page: 67%
Monthly wasted ad spend: R[spend] × 0.67 = R[waste]
```

### Question 4: What offer makes saying no illogical?
Select from the offer progression based on the prospect profile:
- First cold contact → Offer A (Risk Reversal, R7,500)
- Interested but hesitant → Offer B (90-Day Guarantee, R12,500)
- High-value established practice → Offer C (Revenue Share)
- Has existing site → Lead with audit + automation services

The offer must address the specific pain, not be generic.

### Question 5: What is the one frictionless next step?
Not "let me know what you think." One specific, low-effort action:
- "Can I call you for 5 minutes today?"
- "Takes 30 seconds to check on your phone — here's the link"
- "Reply YES and I'll send the full breakdown"

If the next step requires effort from the prospect, it will not happen.

## SECTION 3 — PROSPECT SCORING MATRIX

Score every prospect on five dimensions (1-5 each, max 25):

### PAIN SCORE (1-5) — How much are they visibly losing?
| Score | Signal | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 5 | Running ads to a broken page | R4,000+/month calculable daily waste |
| 4 | High reviews, zero/broken website | 5.0 stars, 6 reviews, services page 500 error |
| 3 | Weak site, active social media | Instagram posts regularly, website is template from 2018 |
| 2 | Outdated site, small following | Site works but looks dated, few online signals |
| 1 | Decent site, minor gaps | Functional site, missing some conversion elements |

### URGENCY SCORE (1-5) — How time-sensitive is their pain?
| Score | Signal | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 5 | Domain expiring, competitor just launched, negative review spiral | Their domain is about to lapse, or a competitor just went live with a new site |
| 4 | Active ad spend being wasted now | Every day they run ads to a weak page costs money |
| 3 | Seasonal business approaching peak | Dental practices get busy before school holidays |
| 2 | Steady state with slow leakage | Losing patients slowly, no acute trigger |
| 1 | No urgency signal detected | Business is stable, no visible pressure |

### ACCESSIBILITY SCORE (1-5) — How easy to reach the decision maker?
| Score | Signal | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 5 | Owner-operated, WhatsApp visible, responds on social | Solo dentist with mobile number on Google Maps |
| 4 | Small team, direct number available | Practice manager answers phone, owner nearby |
| 3 | Front desk but owner reachable | Receptionist will pass the message |
| 2 | Gatekeeper, no direct line | Corporate-style reception, owner shielded |
| 1 | Corporate, franchise HQ decides | Multi-location, decision made at head office |

### CONVERTIBILITY SCORE (1-5) — How likely to say yes?
| Score | Signal | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 5 | No website at all | Zero-risk offer is obviously valuable — they have nothing |
| 4 | Terrible site with broken pages | Demo is visually and functionally better in every way |
| 3 | Mediocre site | Needs strong ROI argument to justify switching |
| 2 | Decent site | Must lead with system (automation, reviews) not website |
| 1 | Good site | Only approach with add-on services, not a rebuild |

### RETENTION SCORE (1-5) — Long-term client potential?
| Score | Signal | Example |
|-------|--------|---------|
| 5 | Multi-dentist practice, high volume, multiple medical aids | Will need ongoing optimisation, reports, review automation |
| 4 | Growing business, adding services/hours | Trajectory suggests increasing digital needs |
| 3 | Stable solo practice | Retainer likely for maintenance + reporting |
| 2 | Small operation, limited budget | May do once-off only |
| 1 | One-time project likely | Fix and forget, no ongoing relationship |

### TOTAL SCORE ROUTING

| Score | Priority | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| 20-25 | **PRIORITY** | Build demo immediately. Personal WhatsApp from Max. |
| 15-19 | **HIGH** | Build demo this week. Outreach from Max or Tristan. |
| 10-14 | **MEDIUM** | Add to pipeline. Build when capacity allows. |
| Below 10 | **PASS** | Not worth the time right now. Revisit if signals change. |

### Example: Werner Joubert Dental
| Dimension | Score | Reasoning |
|-----------|-------|-----------|
| Pain | 4 | 5.0 stars, 6 reviews, services page 500 error, no form, no phone on mobile |
| Urgency | 2 | Steady practice, no acute trigger — but every day the site is broken is a missed patient |

| Retention | 3 | Solo practice but uses advanced tech (CEREC, BIOLASE) — likely to value ongoing updates |
| **TOTAL** | **18** | **HIGH** — Build demo this week, personal outreach from Max |

## SECTION 4 — OFFER SELECTION LOGIC

```
INPUT: prospect profile, signal type, relationship status

IF first_contact AND no_prior_relationship:
    → Offer A (Risk Reversal — R7,500)
    → "I built your practice a site. If you love it, R7,500. If not, delete my number."

IF prospect_replied_positively BUT hesitating_on_price:
    → Offer B (90-Day Growth Guarantee — R12,500)
    → "5 new enquiries in 90 days or I work free until you get them."

IF high_value_practice AND established AND 20+_patients_month:
    → Offer C (Revenue Share — R500/patient above baseline)
    → "You pay nothing upfront. I take R500 per new patient above your current baseline."

IF prospect_has_existing_site_they_like:
    → Lead with automation/reviews system (R1,500-R2,500/month)
    → "Your site is fine — but are you getting reviews automatically? Let me show you what that looks like."

IF prospect_says_too_expensive:
    → Reframe as ROI: "Two new patients and it pays for itself."
    → If still resistant → offer split payment: 50% now, 50% on launch
    → Never discount — adjust scope instead

IF prospect_says_nephew_is_building:
    → Do not push. Leave demo link. Follow up in 30 days.
    → "Go for it. The demo's at [URL] if you want a second opinion later."

IF prospect_says_they_dont_need_a_website:
    → Pivot to Google Business Profile optimisation (R2,500 once-off)
    → Or Google Reviews Growth System (R1,500/month standalone)
    → "That's fair. But can I ask — how many new enquiries do you get per month through Google?"
```

## SECTION 5 — THE INNOVATION STANDARD

Every prospect interaction must contain at least one genuinely surprising insight. This is the difference between "some guy offering to build me a website" and "this person knows more about my business than I do."

### What qualifies as a surprising insight:
- A specific technical failure on their site (500 error, broken form, missing phone)
- A competitor comparison they didn't know about (competitor ranks higher despite fewer reviews)
- A quantified cost they never calculated (R4,020/month in wasted ad spend)
- A missed opportunity they hadn't considered (no Saturday hours listed despite being open Saturdays)
- A review they didn't know existed (negative review they haven't responded to)

### How to find insights:
1. **Firecrawl scrape** — extract everything, look for broken pages, missing elements, load speed
2. **Google Maps** — compare their listing to the top 3 competitors (reviews, photos, hours, website)
3. **Google search** — check if they rank for their own name, check competitor rankings
4. **Call their practice** — at lunch hour and after hours. Does anyone answer? What's the voicemail?
5. **Facebook Ad Library** — are they running ads? Where do they go?

### The test:
If the prospect reads your message and thinks "how does he know that?" — the insight is strong enough. If they think "yeah, I know" — find a better one.

## SECTION 6 — QUALITY GATES

Before any outreach is sent, every item must be checked:

### Pre-Outreach Checklist
- [ ] All five questions answered with specific numbers (not estimates, not "approximately")
- [ ] Prospect scored 10+ on the scoring matrix (below 10 = do not pursue)
- [ ] Demo URL confirmed live and QA script returning PASSED
- [ ] All images loading (curl verify hero.jpg, service images)
- [ ] Tally form working (form ID WO0YgJ, source param set)
- [ ] Offer structure selected with one-line justification
- [ ] Surprising insight identified and included in message
- [ ] Outreach message leads with problem, not product
- [ ] Risk is entirely on our side (buyer pays nothing unless they choose to)
- [ ] Next step is one frictionless action (5-minute call or 30-second URL check)
- [ ] WhatsApp message is under 300 words (any longer and they won't read it)
- [ ] Email subject line references their specific problem (not generic)
- [ ] Screenshot of demo hero ready to attach to WhatsApp

### Pre-Call Checklist (if prospect books a call)
- [ ] Re-read their website and Google listing — look for anything that changed
- [ ] Prepare 3 specific things their demo does better than their current site
- [ ] Know their review count, rating, and one specific reviewer by name
- [ ] Know their hours, address, and services without looking
- [ ] Have a price ready but do not mention it until they ask
- [ ] Prepare the one-line close: "Two new patients and it pays for itself"

## SECTION 7 — PIPELINE MANAGEMENT

### Weekly cadence
Every Friday, answer these three questions:

1. **How many demos did we send this week?** (Target: 5)
2. **How many positive responses?** (Target: 2-3)
3. **How many calls booked or payments received?** (Target: 1)

If demos sent < 5 → the bottleneck is prospecting. Scan more areas.
If responses < 2 → the bottleneck is message quality. Review insights and copy.
If calls < 1 → the bottleneck is follow-up. Run the Day 2/5/10 sequence.

### Prospect status tracking
Every prospect must be in one of these states:

| State | Definition | Next Action |
|-------|-----------|-------------|
| IDENTIFIED | Signal detected, scored, not yet contacted | Build demo |
| DEMO BUILT | Demo live, not yet sent | Send outreach |
| CONTACTED | Outreach sent, awaiting response | Follow-up Day 2 |
| ENGAGED | Replied positively, conversation ongoing | Book 5-minute call |
| CALL BOOKED | Call scheduled | Prepare per pre-call checklist |
| NEGOTIATING | Discussing terms/pricing | Present specific offer |
| CLOSED WON | Payment received | Deliver and onboard |
| CLOSED LOST | Declined or went silent after full sequence | Archive, revisit in 90 days |

### The one rule of pipeline
**Never have more demos built than sent.** If you built 3 demos and only sent 1, the system is blocked. Sending is the bottleneck — always.

## SECTION 8 — CHAINING THE SKILLS

When this skill is activated, it orchestrates the other skills in this order:

```
1. SIGNAL DETECTION (7thsense-signal-detection)
   → Identify the prospect and their specific problem signal
   → Output: business name, signal type, signal score

2. OPPORTUNITY SCANNER (7thsense-opportunity-scanner)
   → Scrape their site, score their landing page, find competitors
   → Output: landing page score, competitor comparison, surprising insight

3. ROI CALCULATOR (7thsense-roi-calculator)
   → Calculate the monthly cost of their problem
   → Output: monthly cost in rands, annual cost, one-number pitch line

4. PROSPECT SCORING (this skill, Section 3)
   → Score across all 5 dimensions
   → Output: total score, priority routing

5. OFFER ARCHITECT (7thsense-offer-architect)
   → Select and customise the right offer for this prospect
   → Output: offer name, price, guarantee terms, one-sentence pitch

6. OUTREACH (templates from ~/agency-templates/outreach/)
   → Write the personalised WhatsApp and email messages
   → Output: ready-to-send messages with demo URL

7. QUALITY GATES (this skill, Section 6)
   → Verify all checklist items before approving outreach
   → Output: GO or NO-GO with reasons
```

Every step feeds into the next. No step can be skipped. If any step produces insufficient data, loop back and gather more before proceeding.

## ACTIVATION TRIGGERS

This skill should be activated when:
- Max says "find me a prospect" or "who should we target next"
- A new business is being evaluated for a demo build
- An outreach message is being written or reviewed
- A follow-up strategy is needed for a contacted prospect
- The weekly pipeline review is happening
- Any strategic decision about where to spend build time

This skill should NOT be activated for:
- Actual HTML/CSS building (use locked-components and build skills)
- Visual design decisions (use visual-system and visual-design)
- Technical SEO or performance (use seo-technical)
